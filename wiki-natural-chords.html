Understanding Natural Chords

Diatonic Chords in Major Keys

What Are Natural Chords?

Natural chords (also called diatonic chords) are chords built using only notes from a single major scale. Every major key has exactly 7 natural chords.

The Natural Chord Pattern

In ANY major key, the natural chords always follow this pattern:

Major - minor - minor - Major - Major - minor - diminished
I - ii - iii - IV - V - vi - vii°
💡 The Pattern Never Changes!
Major - minor - minor - Major - Major - minor - diminished
I - ii - iii - IV - V - vi - vii°

This pattern is the same in EVERY major key. C major, G major, F# major - they all follow this exact pattern!

Example: Natural Chords in C Major

Position Roman Numeral Quality Chord Name Notes
1st I Major C C - E - G
2nd ii minor Dm D - F - A
3rd iii minor Em E - G - B
4th IV Major F F - A - C
5th V Major G G - B - D
6th vi minor Am A - C - E
7th vii° diminished Bdim B - D - F

Why "Natural"?

These chords are called "natural" because:

  • All notes come from one scale: No sharps or flats outside the key
  • Built with skip-pick: Created by stacking every other scale note
  • Sound harmonious: They naturally work well together
  • Most progressions use these: 90% of pop/rock uses only natural chords
🎹 Quick Check:
• If the Roman numeral matches the pattern above = Natural chord
• If it doesn't match (like VI major instead of vi minor) = Borrowed chord
