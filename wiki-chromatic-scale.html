The Chromatic Scale (All 12 Notes)

These are all the notes available on a keyboard, repeating every octave:

C C# D D# E F F# G G# A A# B

White boxes = white keys. Black boxes = black keys.

What is the Chromatic Scale?

The chromatic scale contains all 12 notes in Western music. It includes:

  • 7 natural notes: C, D, E, F, G, A, B (white keys)
  • 5 sharp/flat notes: C#/Dέβφ, D#/Eέβφ, F#/Gέβφ, G#/Aέβφ, A#/Bέβφ (black keys)

Enharmonic Equivalents

Notice that each black key has two names. These are called enharmonic equivalents - different names for the same sound:

Black keys have two names:

έΑλ C# = Dέβφ (same key, two names)
έΑλ D# = Eέβφ
έΑλ F# = Gέβφ
έΑλ G# = Aέβφ
έΑλ A# = Bέβφ

Use sharps (#) when going up, flats (έβφ) when going down!

The Natural Half Steps

Look at the keyboard diagram - notice there's no black key between:

  • E and F - These are naturally a half step apart
  • B and C - These are naturally a half step apart

This is why the W-W-H-W-W-W-H pattern works! The H (half steps) naturally fall on EέΗΤF and BέΗΤC in the C major scale.

Understanding Steps

Step Definitions:

έΑλ Half Step (H): Move to the very next key (white or black)
  Examples: CέΗΤC#, EέΗΤF, BέΗΤC

έΑλ Whole Step (W): Skip one key, land on the next
  Examples: CέΗΤD (skip C#), DέΗΤE (skip D#), FέΗΤG (skip F#)

Why 12 Notes?

The chromatic scale divides the octave into 12 equal parts (semitones). This gives us:

  • Maximum flexibility: We can build scales starting from any of the 12 notes
  • Rich harmony: 12 notes provide enough variety for interesting chords and progressions
  • Modulation: We can smoothly transition between different keys
ΏθΟ╣ Memory Tip: The chromatic scale from C is just every single key going up:
C - C# - D - D# - E - F - F# - G - G# - A - A# - B - C
That's it! All 12 notes in order.
ΏθΤκ Visual Pattern: On a piano, the black keys come in groups of 2 and 3:
έΑλ Group of 2: C# and D# (between C-D-E)
έΑλ Group of 3: F#, G#, and A# (between F-G-A-B)
This pattern repeats across the entire keyboard!
