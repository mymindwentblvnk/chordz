Pro Tips and Practice

Level up your playing

Pro Tips

💡 Use the Search Chords Tool!
The easiest way: Select your root note in Search Chords, and it shows you exactly which notes to play for each chord!

Practice Tips

  1. Start Simple: Begin with C major - it uses only white keys!
  2. Learn Patterns: Once you know C major chords, other keys use the same finger patterns.
  3. Use Inversions: You don't always need to play the root note on bottom. Try different voicings.
  4. Play Along: Use the pitch detector to verify you're playing the right notes.
  5. Practice Progressions: Pick a simple progression (like I-IV-V-I) and play it in multiple keys.

Learning Strategies

Week 1: Master the W-W-H-W-W-W-H pattern
• Build C, G, and F major scales
• Practice until you can do it without thinking

Week 2: Build chords using skip-pick
• Build I, IV, V in C major
• Add ii, iii, vi chords

Week 3: Learn progressions
• Practice I-V-vi-IV in C major
• Transpose to G and D major

Week 4: Experiment with inversions
• Try different voicings of the same chord
• Notice how smooth movement improves sound

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping theory: Understanding WHY helps you learn faster
  • Only practicing C major: You need to transpose to truly understand
  • Ignoring inversions: They make your playing sound professional
  • Not using tools: The Calculate Chords and Search Chords tools are your friends!
  • Rushing: Take time to understand each concept before moving on

Next Steps

Once you're comfortable with the basics:

  • Explore borrowed chords for more color
  • Learn minor scales for darker sounds
  • Practice chord progressions from Search Chords
  • Use the pitch detector to find your root note
🎹 Final Tip: Music theory isn't about memorization - it's about understanding patterns. Focus on the patterns (like W-W-H-W-W-W-H and skip-pick) and everything else follows naturally!
